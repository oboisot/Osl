to\+Ellipsoid 

Transform a \mbox{\hyperlink{classOsl_1_1Geography_1_1GeoPoint}{Geo\+Point}} from a reference ellipsoid to another.

The transformation is made through Helmert\textquotesingle{}s formulation which is a 7-\/parameters similitude transform. The algorithm of the transform is as follows\+:


\begin{DoxyEnumerate}
\item Transform geodetic coordinates to cartesian coordinates\+:

\[ (\lambda_1,\phi_1,h_1)\rightarrow(X_1,Y_1,Z_1) \]

This is made by default in the initialization of a \mbox{\hyperlink{classOsl_1_1Geography_1_1GeoPoint}{Geo\+Point}}.
\item Compute the similitude transform\+:

\[ \begin{pmatrix}X_2 \\ Y_2 \\ Z_2\end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix}T_x \\ T_y \\ T_z\end{pmatrix} + (1+s) \mathbf{R}\begin{pmatrix}X_1 \\ Y_1 \\ Z_1\end{pmatrix} \]

with $\overrightarrow{T}=(T_x,T_y,T_z)$ is the translation from \mbox{\hyperlink{classOsl_1_1Geography_1_1Ellipsoid}{Ellipsoid}} 1 to \mbox{\hyperlink{classOsl_1_1Geography_1_1Ellipsoid}{Ellipsoid}} 2, $s$ is the scale factor of the transformation and $\mathbf{R}$ is the rotation matrix between referential 1 and referential 2. This rotation matrix can be taken exact\+:

\[ \mathbf{R}(\theta_x,\theta_y,\theta_z)= R_x(\theta_x)R_y(\theta_y)R_z(\theta_z) \]

where $R_x$, $R_y$ and $R_z$ are the elementary rotation matrix of angles $\theta_x$, $\theta_y$ and $\theta_z$ around axes $x$, $y$ and $z$, respectively. This rotation matrix is often approximated by its small angles version\+:

\[ R=\begin{pmatrix} 1 & -\theta_z & \theta_y \\ \theta_z & 1 & -\theta_x \\ -\theta_y & \theta_x & 1 \\ \end{pmatrix} \]
\item Once the new geocentric coordinates $(X_2,Y_2,Z_2)$ are computed the corresponding geodetic coordinates are estimated\+:

\[ (X_2,Y_2,Z_2)\rightarrow(\lambda_2,\phi_2,h_2) \]
\end{DoxyEnumerate}
