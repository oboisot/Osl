Convert geodetic coordinates from decimal degrees to Degrees-\/\+Minutes-\/\+Seconds (D\+MS). 

\DoxyHorRuler{0}
 This function allows to convert geodetic coordinates from decimal degrees to Degrees-\/\+Minutes-\/\+Seconds (D\+MS).

Decimal degrees $dd$ are converted to D\+MS coordinates $d$, $m$, $s$ following the algorithm\+:


\begin{DoxyEnumerate}
\item $d = \big\lfloor\left\vert dd\right\vert\big\rfloor$
\item $m_d = 60\left(\left\vert dd\right\vert-d\right)$
\item $m = \big\lfloor m_t\big\rfloor$
\item $s = 60\left(m_t-m\right)$
\item $d = \mathrm{sign}(dd)d$
\end{DoxyEnumerate}

Convert geodetic coordinates from Degrees-\/\+Minutes-\/\+Seconds (D\+MS) to decimal degrees. 

\DoxyHorRuler{0}
 This function allows to convert geodetic coordinates from Degrees-\/\+Minutes-\/\+Seconds (D\+MS) to decimal degrees. The orientation of the coordinates (North-\/\+South, or West-\/\+East) are taken into account through the sign of the {\itshape d} (degree) parameter with +1 for North or East orientation and -\/1 for South or West orientation.

D\+MS coordinates ( $d$, $m$, $s$) are converted to decimal degrees $dd$ through the relationship\+:

\[ dd=\mathrm{sign}(d)\left(\vert d\vert+\dfrac{m}{60}+\dfrac{s}{3600}\right) \]
