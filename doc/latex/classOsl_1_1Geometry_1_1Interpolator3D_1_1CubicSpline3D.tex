\doxysubsection{Detailed Description}
Class to construct a piecewise Cubic Spline interpolator of a 3D trajectory. 

\DoxyHorRuler{0}
 \doxyparagraph*{Principle}

Knowing position and velocity vectors at a given time $t$, this class defines a physical interpolator of the trajectory based on underlying Cubic Spline interpolators for each x, y and z coordinates.

Let a set of temporal points $(t_k)_{k\in[\vert0;N\vert]}$ of size $N+1$ being in a strictly increasing order\+:

\[ t_0<t_1<\cdots<t_{n-1}<t_n \]

with their corresponding position $(x_k, y_k, z_k)_{k\in[\vert0;N\vert]}$ and velocity $(v_{x,k}, v_{y,k}, v_{z,k})_{k\in[\vert0;N\vert]}$ vectors.

The piecewise Cubic Spline interpolation functions at index $k$ for coordinates x, y and z are defined by the third order polynomials\+:

\[ \begin{split} f_{x,k}(t) & =a_{x,k}(t-t_k)^3+b_{x,k}(t-t_k)^2+c_{x,k}(t-t_k)+d_{x,k} \\ f_{y,k}(t) & =a_{y,k}(t-t_k)^3+b_{y,k}(t-t_k)^2+c_{y,k}(t-t_k)+d_{y,k} \\ f_{z,k}(t) & =a_{z,k}(t-t_k)^3+b_{z,k}(t-t_k)^2+c_{z,k}(t-t_k)+d_{z,k} \end{split} ,\quad\forall t\in[t_k; t_{k+1}[\quad ; \quad\forall k\in[\vert0;N-1\vert] \]

providing a set of $3\times N$ interpolation functions with their corresponding coefficients\+:

\[ \begin{split} (f_{x,k})_{k\in[\vert0;N-1\vert]} & \mbox{ with } (a_{x,k}, b_{x,k}, c_{x,k}, d_{x,k})_{k\in[\vert0;N-1\vert]} \\ (f_{y,k})_{k\in[\vert0;N-1\vert]} & \mbox{ with } (a_{y,k}, b_{y,k}, c_{y,k}, d_{y,k})_{k\in[\vert0;N-1\vert]} \\ (f_{z,k})_{k\in[\vert0;N-1\vert]} & \mbox{ with } (a_{z,k}, b_{z,k}, c_{z,k}, d_{z,k})_{k\in[\vert0;N-1\vert]} \\ \end{split} \]

\doxyparagraph*{Computation of the interpolator coefficients}

Coefficients are determined by ensuring continuity of the interpolation functions (position vectors) and their first derivative (velocity vectors) at each interval nodes.

Considering the case of the $x$ coordinate, the first derivative with respect to variable $t$ of the piecewise cubic spline is given by\+:

\[ f\textnormal{\textquotesingle}_{x,k}(x)=3a_{x,k}(t-t_k)^2+2b_{x,k}(t-t_k)+c_{x,k},\quad \forall t\in[t_k; t_{k+1}[\quad ; \quad\forall k\in[\vert0;N-1\vert] \]

Continuity of the position and velocity vectors results in the sytem of equations for the $x$ coordinate\+:

\[ \begin{array}{rcl} f_{x,k}(t_k) & = & x_k\\ f\textnormal{\textquotesingle}_{x,k}(t_k) & = & v_{x,k} \\ f_{x,k}(t_{k+1}) & = & x_{k+1}\\ f\textnormal{\textquotesingle}_{x,k}(t_{k+1}) & = & v_{x,k+1} \\ \end{array} \]

Solving this system results in the evaluation of the coefficients for the piecewise cubic interpolation functions $f_{x,k}$\+:

\[ \left\{\begin{array}{rcl} a_{x,k} & = & \dfrac{1}{(t_{k+1}-t_k)^2}\left(-2 \dfrac{x_{k+1}-x_k} {t_{k+1}-t_k}+v_{x,k}+v_{x,k+1}\right) \\ b_{x,k} & = & \dfrac{1}{t_{k+1}-t_k}\left(3 \dfrac{x_{k+1}-x_k} {t_{k+1}-t_k}-2v_{x,k}-v_{x,k+1}\right) \\ c_{x,k} & = & v_{x,k} \\ d_{x,k} & = & x_k \end{array}\right., \quad\forall k\in[\vert0;N-1\vert] \]

The same procedure is applied for y and z coordinates.
