\doxysubsection{Detailed Description}
Class to construct a piecewise Cubic Spline interpolator from complex data. 

\DoxyHorRuler{0}
 \doxyparagraph*{Principle}

This class defines a one dimensional piecewise cubic spline interpolator. Let a set of evaluation points $(x_k)_{k\in[\vert0;N\vert]}$ of size $N+1$ being in a strictly increasing order\+:

\[ x_0<x_1<\cdots<x_{n-1}<x_n \]

The piecewise cubic spline interpolation function at index $k$ is defined by the third order polynomial\+:

\[ f_k(x)=a_k(x-x_k)^3+b_k(x-x_k)^2+c_k(x-x_k)+d_k,\quad \forall x\in[x_k; x_{k+1}[\quad ; \quad\forall k\in[\vert0;N-1\vert] \]

providing a set of $N$ interpolation functions $(f_k)_{k\in[\vert0;N-1\vert]}$ with their corresponding sets of coefficients $(a_k, b_k, c_k, d_k)_{k\in[\vert0;N-1\vert]}$.

\doxyparagraph*{Computation of the interpolator coefficients}

This class provides two main cubic spline interpolators depending on the available data, to know\+:


\begin{DoxyItemize}
\item A {\bfseries{Cubic Hermite Spline}} when evaluation points $x$, function values $y=f(x)$ and function first derivative values $y\textnormal{\textquotesingle}=f\textnormal{\textquotesingle}(x)$ are given.

Expression of the first derivative of the piecewise cubic spline is simply given by\+:

\[ f\textnormal{\textquotesingle}_k(x)=3a_k(x-x_k)^2+2b_k(x-x_k)+c_k,\quad \forall x\in[x_k; x_{k+1}[\quad ; \quad\forall k\in[\vert0;N-1\vert] \]

Coefficients are thus determined by ensuring continuity of the interpolation function and its first derivative at each interval nodes, resulting in the sytem of equations\+:

\[ \begin{array}{rcl} f_k(x_k) & = & y_k\\ f\textnormal{\textquotesingle}_k(x_k) & = & y\textnormal{\textquotesingle}_k \\ f_k(x_{k+1}) & = & y_{k+1}\\ f\textnormal{\textquotesingle}_k(x_{k+1}) & = & y\textnormal{\textquotesingle}_{k+1} \\ \end{array} \]

Solving this system results in the evaluation of the coefficients for the piecewise cubic interpolation functions $f_k$\+:

\[ \left\{\begin{array}{rcl} a_k & = & \dfrac{1}{(x_{k+1}-x_k)^2}\left(-2 \dfrac{y_{k+1}-y_k}{x_{k+1}-x_k}+y\textnormal{\textquotesingle}_k+y\textnormal{\textquotesingle}_{k+1}\right) \\ b_k & = & \dfrac{1}{x_{k+1}-x_k}\left(3 \dfrac{y_{k+1}-y_k}{x_{k+1}-x_k}-2y\textnormal{\textquotesingle}_k-y\textnormal{\textquotesingle}_{k+1}\right) \\ c_k & = & y\textnormal{\textquotesingle}_k \\ d_k & = & y_k \end{array}\right., \quad\forall k\in[\vert0;N-1\vert] \]
\item A {\bfseries{Cubic Spline}} when evaluation points $x$ and function values at these points are given $y=f(x)$. The cubic spline interpolant satisfies the following conditions\+:
\begin{DoxyEnumerate}
\item $f_k(x_k)=y_k$ ({\itshape value at nodes})
\item $f_{k+1}(x_{k+1})=f_k(x_{k+1})$ ({\itshape continuity at nodes})
\item $f\textnormal{\textquotesingle}_{k+1}(x_{k+1})=f\textnormal{\textquotesingle}_k(x_{k+1})$ ({\itshape first derivative continuity at nodes})
\item $f\textnormal{\textquotesingle}\textnormal{\textquotesingle}_{k+1}(x_{k+1})=f\textnormal{\textquotesingle}\textnormal{\textquotesingle}_k(x_{k+1})$ ({\itshape second derivative continuity at nodes})
\end{DoxyEnumerate}

Those relationships can be rewritten into the following system for the coefficients of the piecewise cubic spline interpolators (see \cite{Burden_10} pages 146-\/148)\+:

\[ \begin{array}{rclr} d_k & = & y_k & \mbox{ (1)}\\ h_{k-1}b_{k-1}+2(h_{k-1}+h_k)b_k+h_kb_{k+1} & = & \dfrac{3}{h_k}(y_{k+1}-y_k)-\dfrac{3}{h_{k-1}}(y_k-y_{k-1}) & \mbox{ (2)} \\ c_k & = & \dfrac{y_{k+1}-y_k}{h_k}-\dfrac{h_k}{3}(2b_k+b_{k+1}) & \mbox{ (3)} \\ a_k & = & \dfrac{b_{k+1}-b_k}{3h_k} & \mbox{ (4)} \end{array} \]

where $h_k=x_{k+1}-x_k,\quad\forall k\in[\vert0;N-1\vert]$. Equation (1) finds the $d_k$ coefficients and is simply given by data, equation (2) requires to solve a linear system to estimate the $b_k$ coefficients. Once all $b_k$ coefficients are determined, coefficients $c_k$ and $a_k$ are determined through equations (3) and (4), respectively.

System of equation (2) can be written in a matrix form as\+:

\[ A\mathbf{x}=\mathbf{b} \] with\+:

\[ A=\begin{pmatrix} - & - & - & - & - & - \\ h_0 & 2(h_0+h_1) & h_1 & 0 & \cdots & 0 \\ 0 & h_1 & 2(h_1+h_2) & h_2 & \ddots & \vdots \\ \vdots & \ddots & \ddots & \ddots & \ddots & 0 \\ 0 & \cdots & 0 & h_{n-2} & 2(h_{n-2}+h_{n-1}) & h_{n-1} \\ - & - & - & - & - & - \end{pmatrix} \]

\[ \mathbf{x}=\begin{pmatrix} b_0 \\ b_1 \\ \vdots \\ b_n \end{pmatrix} \quad\mbox{and}\quad \mathbf{b}=\begin{pmatrix} - \\ \dfrac{3}{h_1}(y_2-y_1)-\dfrac{3}{h_0}(d_1-d_0) \\ \vdots \\ \dfrac{3}{h_{n-1}}(y_n-y_{n-1})-\dfrac{3}{h_{n-2}}(y_{n-1}-y_{n-2}) \\ - \end{pmatrix} \]

However, equation (2) gives only access to $N-1$ equations for a $N+1$ system of equations (see matrix $A$ and vector $\mathbf{b}$), two more equations are thus required to solve this system.

This can be achieved by imposing two {\bfseries{boundary conditions}} on the cubic spline interpolators, the most classical are\+:
\begin{DoxyItemize}
\item The {\bfseries{Natural}} or {\bfseries{Free}} boundary which supposes that the second derivative of the function at first and last points are equal to zero\+: \[ f\textnormal{\textquotesingle}\textnormal{\textquotesingle}_0(x_0)=f\textnormal{\textquotesingle}\textnormal{\textquotesingle}_n(x_n)=0 \] resulting in the two supplementary equations\+: \[ b_0=b_n=0 \] Using these two equations, matrix $A$ is thus equal to\+: \[ A=\begin{pmatrix} 1 & 0 & 0 & \cdots & \cdots & 0 \\ h_0 & 2(h_0+h_1) & h_1 & \ddots & & \vdots \\ 0 & h_1 & 2(h_1+h_2) & h_2 & \ddots & \vdots \\ \vdots & \ddots & \ddots & \ddots & \ddots & 0 \\ \vdots & & \ddots & h_{n-2} & 2(h_{n-2}+h_{n-1}) & h_{n-1} \\ 0 & \cdots & \cdots & 0 & 0 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \] and vector $\mathbf{b}$ to\+: \[ \mathbf{b}=\begin{pmatrix} 0 \\ \dfrac{3}{h_1}(y_2-y_1)-\dfrac{3}{h_0}(d_1-d_0) \\ \vdots \\ \dfrac{3}{h_{n-1}}(y_n-y_{n-1})-\dfrac{3}{h_{n-2}}(y_{n-1}-y_{n-2}) \\ 0 \end{pmatrix} \] The estimation of the piecewise cubic polynomials coefficients requires the inversion of a tridiagonal matrix and follows algorithm 3.\+4 pages 149-\/150 of \cite{Burden_10}.
\item The {\bfseries{Quadratic}} boundary defines the first and last piecewise polynomials as quadratic polynomials, imposing that\+: \[ a_0=a_{n-1}=0 \] in order to get quadratic polynomials for the first and last interpolator. Plugging these conditions in equation (4), we obtain the matrix $A$\+: \[ A=\begin{pmatrix} 1 & -1 & 0 & \cdots & \cdots & 0 \\ h_0 & 2(h_0+h_1) & h_1 & \ddots & & \vdots \\ 0 & h_1 & 2(h_1+h_2) & h_2 & \ddots & \vdots \\ \vdots & \ddots & \ddots & \ddots & \ddots & 0 \\ \vdots & & \ddots & h_{n-2} & 2(h_{n-2}+h_{n-1}) & h_{n-1} \\ 0 & \cdots & \cdots & 0 & -1 & 1 \end{pmatrix} \] and the corresponding vector $\mathbf{b}$\+: \[ \mathbf{b}=\begin{pmatrix} 0 \\ \dfrac{3}{h_1}(y_2-y_1)-\dfrac{3}{h_0}(d_1-d_0) \\ \vdots \\ \dfrac{3}{h_{n-1}}(y_n-y_{n-1})-\dfrac{3}{h_{n-2}}(y_{n-1}-y_{n-2}) \\ 0 \end{pmatrix} \] Matrix $A$ is a tridiagonal matrix and the Crout factorization for tridiagonal linear systems presented in algorithm 6.\+7 pages 422-\/423 of \cite{Burden_10} is used to estimate the $b_k$ coefficients.
\item The {\bfseries{Not-\/a-\/\+Knot}} boundary which imposes that the third derivative of the function at the two first and two last piecewise polynomials are continuous\+: \[ f\textnormal{\textquotesingle}\textnormal{\textquotesingle}\textnormal{\textquotesingle}_0(x_1)=f\textnormal{\textquotesingle}\textnormal{\textquotesingle}\textnormal{\textquotesingle}_1(x_1)\quad\mbox{and}\quad f\textnormal{\textquotesingle}\textnormal{\textquotesingle}\textnormal{\textquotesingle}_{n-2}(x_{n-1})=f\textnormal{\textquotesingle}\textnormal{\textquotesingle}\textnormal{\textquotesingle}_{n-1}(x_{n-1}) \] resulting in the two supplementary conditions\+: \[ a_0=a_1\quad\mbox{and}\quad a_{n-2}=a_{n-1} \] which, using equation (4), allows to estimate the matrix $A$ as\+: \[ A=\begin{pmatrix} h_1 & -(h_0+h_1) & h_0 & 0 & \cdots & 0 \\ h_0 & 2(h_0+h_1) & h_1 & 0 & & \vdots \\ 0 & h_1 & 2(h_1+h_2) & h_2 & \ddots & \vdots \\ \vdots & \ddots & \ddots & \ddots & \ddots & 0 \\ \vdots & & 0 & h_{n-2} & 2(h_{n-2}+h_{n-1}) & h_{n-1} \\ 0 & \cdots & 0 & h_{n-1} & -(h_{n-2}+h_{n-1}) & h_{n-2} \end{pmatrix} \] and vector $\mathbf{b}$ to\+: \[ \mathbf{b}=\begin{pmatrix} 0 \\ \dfrac{3}{h_1}(y_2-y_1)-\dfrac{3}{h_0}(d_1-d_0) \\ \vdots \\ \dfrac{3}{h_{n-1}}(y_n-y_{n-1})-\dfrac{3}{h_{n-2}}(y_{n-1}-y_{n-2}) \\ 0 \end{pmatrix} \]
\end{DoxyItemize}

Search the index for spline interpolation. 

\DoxyHorRuler{0}
 This function makes use of binary search to find the first index $i$ of reference axis $x[i]$ (in strictly increasing order) such that, for the search value $x_{eval}$\+:

\[ x_{eval} \geq x[i] \]

\hline
\end{DoxyParams}

\end{DoxyReturn}
\begin{DoxyNote}{Note}
1. If $x_{eval}\geq\underset{i}{\max}(x[i])$, last index minus 1 is returned.
