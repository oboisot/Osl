toEllipsoid

Transform a GeoPoint from a reference ellipsoid to another.

The transformation is made through Helmert's formulation which is a 7-parameters similitude transform. The algorithm of the transform is as follows:

  1. Transform geodetic coordinates to cartesian coordinates:

    \[ (\lambda_1,\phi_1,h_1)\rightarrow(X_1,Y_1,Z_1) \]

    This is made by default in the initialization of a GeoPoint.

  2. Compute the similitude transform:

    \[ \begin{pmatrix}X_2 \\ Y_2 \\ Z_2\end{pmatrix} = \begin{pmatrix}T_x \\ T_y \\ T_z\end{pmatrix} + (1+s) \mathbf{R}\begin{pmatrix}X_1 \\ Y_1 \\ Z_1\end{pmatrix} \]

    with \(\overrightarrow{T}=(T_x,T_y,T_z)\) is the translation from Ellipsoid 1 to Ellipsoid 2, \(s\) is the scale factor of the transformation and \(\mathbf{R}\) is the rotation matrix between referential 1 and referential 2. This rotation matrix can be taken exact:

    \[ \mathbf{R}(\theta_x,\theta_y,\theta_z)= R_x(\theta_x)R_y(\theta_y)R_z(\theta_z) \]

    where \(R_x\), \(R_y\) and \(R_z\) are the elementary rotation matrix of angles \(\theta_x\), \(\theta_y\) and \(\theta_z\) around axes \(x\), \(y\) and \(z\), respectively. This rotation matrix is often approximated by its small angles version:

    \[ R=\begin{pmatrix} 1 & -\theta_z & \theta_y \\ \theta_z & 1 & -\theta_x \\ -\theta_y & \theta_x & 1 \\ \end{pmatrix} \]

  3. Once the new geocentric coordinates \((X_2,Y_2,Z_2)\) are computed the corresponding geodetic coordinates are estimated:

    \[ (X_2,Y_2,Z_2)\rightarrow(\lambda_2,\phi_2,h_2) \]
